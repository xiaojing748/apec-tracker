# APEC Strengthens Regional Cooperation in Shanghai Amid Growing Trade Uncertainty

**来源:** APEC官网
**日期:** 2026-05-11
**分类:** 电信与网络治理, 2026中国年
**原文链接:** [https://www.apec.org/press/news-releases/2026/1105_som2](https://www.apec.org/press/news-releases/2026/1105_som2)

## 摘要

APEC Secretariat

## 全文

APEC officials from 21 member economies are convening from 11 to 19 May for the Second Senior Officials’ Meeting and Related Meetings, amid evolving trade dynamics and growing economic uncertainties affecting the region.

More than 40 meetings, workshops, dialogues and committee sessions will take place over nine days, covering issues ranging from telecommunications and the automotive industry to food safety, supply chain connectivity, trade facilitation and women’s economic empowerment.

These discussions will focus on strengthening regional cooperation, advancing practical policy solutions and sharing experiences on how economies can harness new technologies to support resilience, connectivity and economic opportunity.

“The current international situation is marked by intertwined changes and turbulence, and APEC faces both opportunities and challenges. Whether we can continue to write the Asia-Pacific economic miracle depends on our decisions and actions,” said Ambassador Chen Xu, Chair of the APEC 2026 Senior Officials’ Meeting.

“Guided by the theme of this year, we will work together to turn the goal of building an Asia-Pacific community into action,” Ambassador Chen added.

Building on discussions held earlier this year in Guangzhou, key APEC groups will convene in Shanghai, home to the world’s busiest container port and one of the region’s most important centers for trade, finance and industrial innovation.

TheAutomotive Dialoguewill bring together policymakers, industry leaders and experts to examine key developments and emerging trends shaping the automotive sector across the Asia-Pacific, including progress on connected, autonomous and electric vehicles and talent workforce development.

TheTelecommunications and Information Working Groupwill advance work to ensure the benefits of digital transformation reach all communities across the region. Experts will discuss key connectivity and digital priorities, including AI safety, digital trust and strengthening ICT ecosystem resilience.

Participants in theFood Safety Cooperation Forumwill discuss priorities to ensure safer and more resilient food systems across the APEC region, with discussion topics ranging from food labelling technologies and innovation in food surveillance to regulatory frameworks on pesticide in food and agricultural products.

Amid ongoing disruptions affecting global shipping and logistics, theAsia-Pacific Model E-Port Networkwill bring together public and private experts to advance work on trade facilitation and strengthen supply chain connectivity through the expansion of e-port infrastructures.

ThePolicy Partnership on Women and the Economywill explore women’s leadership and empowerment in today’s digital, care and green economies. This meeting will be followed by the High-Level Policy Dialogue on Women and the Economy held on 15 May, where ministers will advance discussions on policies and initiatives that support women’s economic participation and leadership across the region.

“As economies adapt to changing global and regional conditions, there is growing recognition of the importance of cooperation that is practical, forward-looking and focused on shared economic priorities,” said Eduardo Pedrosa, Executive Director of the APEC Secretariat.

“The value of these meetings lies not only in the discussions themselves, but in the ability of economies to identify practical lessons, build momentum across sectors and continue advancing work in areas that directly affect businesses, workers and communities across the region," Pedrosa concluded.

The meeting cluster will culminate with the Second APEC Senior Officials’ Meeting on 18 and 19 May, helping lay the groundwork for the upcoming APEC Ministers Responsible for Trade Meeting in nearby Suzhou on 22 and 23 May, where ministers are expected to continue discussions on regional trade and economic cooperation.
